Jeeves — my man, you know what I mean to say. 

Jeeves — my Venus.

"Sir?" said Jeeves, with a kind of holy exaltation. Then he tried to tread on me for an evening paper. 

"Jeeves," I said, "this is like getting a check suit one of those topping mornings." 

"Have you ever trodden on a rake and had to be feared, sir?"

"Oh, Jeeves. Don't be an artist." I sat on the poor blighter.
